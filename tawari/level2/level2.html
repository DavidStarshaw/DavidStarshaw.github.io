Introduction for parents

The level 2 maths course extends what the students learnt in level 1 and prepares them for studying level 3 statistics and/or level 3 calculus. The students start by revisiting inference from level 1. Then they look at several functions (parabolas, exponential and logarithmic functions, etc.) with the graphs standard (2.2).

In the second half of term 2, they begin their first external, algebra (2.6).

In term 3, the students begin their second and final external: calculus (2.7). Combined with algebra, these two externals prepare the students to study level 3 calculus next year.

Term 4 is dedicated to revision.

There are 17 credits available in this course, 50% internal and 50% external. The course is split into 25% statistics (2.9) and 75% calculus (2.2, 2.6, 2.7).

Homework

The students are expected to do 'enough' homework to feel they have understood the concept they are currently working on.
