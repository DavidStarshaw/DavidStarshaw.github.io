What is this website?

I am a third-year maths teacher at Wā Ora Montessori School in Naenae, New Zealand. I developed these resources (and collated others' resources) with my students in mind but any students or teachers are welcome to use and distribute them according to either the creative commons license at the bottom of the page for my resources or the relevant license for others' resources. Resources that belong to others are always linked to the original source and the author's website if applicable. I can be contacted at david@waora.school.nz, say hi.

Why a creative commons license?

I've appreciated all the help I've had as a beginning teacher in a small school especially when the help has been free or has saved me having to buy resources. And resources can be expensive! I am particularly grateful to several people who have made excellent resources available online for free. I name these people in the acknowledgements below.

So in turn, I wanted to make my resources easily accessible by my students and their parents, as well as free for other students and teachers around New Zealand so I made this website and chose to publish under a creative commons license rather than a copyright license. I wanted a license that would allow people to use either the whole or parts of my resources freely, hence derivatives are allowed. However, I didn't want the resources to be sold, even as part of a derivative work, so the license is also non-commercial, share-alike.

Acknowledgements

A few people have made fantastic resources available for free which I have used often. A large part of my job has been to only bring those resources together into one place. I am indescribably grateful to those people. They are:
  • Jake Wills at MathsNZ. Jake has made fantastic resources for NCEA statistics and also pointed me on my first steps in learning html. You should look at his senior statistics resources and NZGrapher.
  • Brady Haran at Numberphile. Brady has produced a large number of videos that have been equally entertaining and illuminating. Go watch his videos. He has channels for other topics too including computers, chemistry, and physics. They are all worth checking out.
  • Matt Parker at standupmaths. Matt features in many of Brady's Numberphile videos, has written my favourite maths book and is "approximately one-third" of the Festival of the Spoken Nerd. Everything he says is either mathematical, humourous, or both (probably shown in a Venn diagram).
  • Dan Meyer at dy/dan. I was first introduced to Dan through his TED talk which I recommend to anyone. He has a lot of wisdom to share about mathematics teaching and learning. He has also produced excellent resources which I use with gratitude.
  • Dave Cameron and the team at LearnCoach. They have put together an impressively large number of high-quality video tutorials specifically about NCEA standards in maths and the sciences. I am grateful for the work they've put in to creating these resources, they are excellent and I recommend them to anyone studying NCEA maths or science.
