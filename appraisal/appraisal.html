PTC 2

Demonstrate commitment to promote the well-being of all ākonga

  1. take all reasonable steps to provide and maintain a teaching and learning environment that is physically, socially, culturally and emotionally safe.
  2. acknowledge and respect the languages, heritages and cultures of all ākonga
  3. comply with relevant regulatory and statutory requirements.

2017 evidence

  1. At Wā Ora Montessori School, we use a restorative process when dealing with conflict. We support students to identify harm that’s done to them and to empathise with harm that’s done to others. I have facilitated restorative conversations between students in many situations. Often requiring no more than a few minutes but sometimes a longer, more robust process is required. Whenever we talk about reparation needing to be made, we always frame it in terms of the harm done.
  2. I am in contact with the families of my pastoral students when something extraordinarily positive happens or when there’s an issue. If the parents have anything they want to pass on or have a concern, they can contact me and I follow it up for them. I keep a log of every conversation I have with my advisees or their parents so that I can refer to it in the future. It's currently 10 pages long.
  3. I have been supporting one of my advisees who has been off school all term to work from home by liasing with his subject teachers, him, and his family so that he can still be as much of the community as he can without physically being here which will help his reintergration next year.

PTC 3

Demonstrate commitment to bicultural partnership in Aotearoa New Zealand

  1. Demonstrate respect for the heritages, languages and cultures of both partners to the Treaty of Waitangi

2017 evidence

  1. We begin and finish each day with a karakia. A few years before I started at Wā Ora, the Te Reo teacher at that time wrote a karakia for our junior high students (Kawakawa) to finish the day with. The words were never written down but passed on by the students through the years. Over time, the words and pronunciation changed. I worked with our current Te Reo teacher to restore the words and pronunciations to what she thought the original was. They are now written down and the karakia is said properly at the end of each day.
  2. At the start of each year, the junior high students (Kawakawa) go on a two week camp called an odyssey. There are three odysseys that rotate so that in three years, a student has done all three camps. Each odyssey has a tangata whenua component including a noho marae as well as learning about the history of the place and its people. The first odyssey is being in Waitangi for Waitangi day, then following the journey of Te Rauparaha through Tongariro, and eventually to Porirua. The second odyssey looks at historical and modern mining of coal, gold and pounamu. We stay at Arahura marae and learn the history of Ngāti Waewae and pounamu. The third odyssey is based in the Marlborough Sounds and looks at conservation of coastal resources and the seas.

PTC 7

Promote a collaborative, inclusive and supportive learning environment

  1. demonstrate effective management of the learning setting which incorporates successful strategies to engage and motivate ākonga
  2. foster trust, respect and cooperation with and among ākonga

2017 evidence

  1. We have long work cycles at Wā Ora Montessori School. Classes can be as long as three hours. I need to be aware of how my class is, including their energy, focus, and engagement. I use strategies like splitting the lesson into very different parts (e.g. active vs. screen time). I also explicitly teach students to take responsibility for their needs, if they are ‘fading’ then I expect them to step out of the class for 5 or 10 minutes to walk around and clear their heads (though not during a teaching time).
  2. Once a fortnight we have a ‘seminar’ time when the class meets in groups of about 16 students to discuss a text or series of texts that they have read during the week outside of class for about 45 minutes. There are grace and courtesy rules surrounding seminar to facilitate adult-like conversation and to maintain inclusivity.
  3. Each staff meeting, we have a time of ‘pastoral issues’ where we talk about the issues in the classroom at the moment. This ensures all teachers have at least some knowledge of all of the dynamics that we are aware of and what needs to happen to maintain the health of the community.

  3. encourage ākonga to take responsibility for their own learning and behaviour
  4. assist ākonga to think critically about information and ideas and to reflect on their learning

2017 evidence

  1. Because I oversee the maths programme from years 8 to 13, I have a good understanding of what students should know and what they often don't know as they come in to each class. I can use this understanding to make connections and build upon it.
  2. If a student isn't meeting my expectations of them by not bringing equipment to class or not completing work, I start by meeting with them, then their advisor, and sometimes even their parents to assist the student to take responsibility for their own learning
  3. Our restorative process is a valuable process to help students take responsibility for their behaviour. As an advisor, I am heavily involved in this process.
